Two courses on Humans in Software Engineering 8-9 Oct 2018

Two doctoral level courses on Humans in Software Engineering will be organized on Oct 8 and Oct 9 from 08:00-17:00 at Oulu (Hotel Lasaretti). Both courses are worth 1 ETCS and include lectures and homework. Grading: Pass/Fail.  It is recommended that you take both courses but this is by no means mandatory.

 

Registration deadline 2 October

Course A:  Human Aspects of Software Engineering

Speaker: Associate Professor Alexander Serebrenik, University of Eindhoven, The Netherlands <http://www.win.tue.nl/~aserebre/> 

Schedule: 08:00-12:00 (8 Oct) and 08:00-12:00 (9 Oct)

Location:

Room: Linnakabinetti <https://lasaretti.com/en/meetings-parties/venues/linnacabinet/> at  Hotel Lasaretti <https://goo.gl/maps/PA9ZcLsRaDq>  

Contents:

  • Overview of recent results on human aspects in software engineering
  • Foundations of empirical software engineering
  • Practice: application of a quantitative (open coding of survey answers) and a qualitative (statistical analysis) technique to study the use of badges on GitHub
  • Diversity of developer population
  • Automatic identification of demographics (gender, location, age)
  • Practice: empirical evaluation of different gender-guessing techniques
  • Measurement techniques for diversity and inclusion
  • Automatic identification of cognitive (confusion) and affective (emotion) states of software developers
  • Practice: review and design of a simple emotion detector

Course B. Behavioral Software Engineering

Speaker: Professor Robert Feldt, Chalmers University of Technology, Gothenburg, Sweden  <http://www.robertfeldt.net/>

Schedule: 13:00-17:00 (8 Oct) and 13:00-17:00 (9 Oct)

Location:

Room: Linnakabinetti <https://lasaretti.com/en/meetings-parties/venues/linnacabinet/> at  Hotel Lasaretti <https://goo.gl/maps/PA9ZcLsRaDq>  

Contents:

  • Behavioral SE - Introduction and Motivation
  • Why doe we need a new term?
  • Relation to areas of Psychology
  • Individuals in SE - Personality, Motivation, and all that
  • Discussion: Which personalities are common in SE? Does it correlate with SE roles and interests?
  • Teams in SE - Maturity, Identity, Values, Norms, and all that
  • Discussion: Does team maturity correlate with level of agility? Can one mature more quickly? Can we put together
  • Collective Intelligence in SE - When is the whole more than the sum of the parts?
  • Practice (if there is time): Measuring the CI of teams in the class
  • Organisations in SE - Values, Culture, Org misalignment, and all that
  • Discussion: Why is there often organisational misalignment when introducing agile practices?
