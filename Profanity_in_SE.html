The Most Popular Swear Words in Software Engineering

    Abstract

    Software engineers are polite and respectful especially towards females and sexual minorities. Due to the limited use of curse words in software engineering, their use in sentiment or emotion detection has limited applicability. However, such behavior also suggests that dating a software engineer might be a good idea :)

    Introduction

    A recent study listed the top 15 curse words used in Facebook [1] and another study listed the top 9 used in Twitter [2]. Here we study the top profanities in software engineering (SE) and reflect the differences to Facebook and Twitter.

    Data

    I used data set by Ortu et al. [3] which contains roughly 700,000 Jira issue reports, 2 million comments from one thousand projects. Altogether the total number of words in the data is 220,931,713 with 2,377,708 unique words. Some notable projects in the data set are for example Hadoop, and Cassandra.

    Results

    The table below shows the most popular curse words using the top 15 curse words of Facebook and the top 9 curse words of Twitter as our lexicon. We see that the total number of curse words used is very low. In total, the top curse words are used only 2,824 times which means that the proportion of curse words is only 0.0013% of the text in total

    We can learn that the top five curse words are "hell", "damn", "crap", "ass", and "shit". This means that software engineers are using the nicer curse words if we compare it with the Facebook and Twitter users who have higher preference for the F-word. There is also respect towards the sexual minorities as "fag" is mentioned only four times. Insults towards women are also quite limited, "bitch" is mentioned 72 times,"whore" 4 times and finally "slut" is not mentioned at all
