Harmonic Ranking of Finnish Software Engineering Scholars

Some people believe that multi author papers unfairly increase the size of the publication lists of secondary authors. This article (at PLOS one) suggests using harmonic authorship counting to prevent such bias. In harmonic counting authorship credits are divided so that first author always receives the most credit. This is illustrated in the figure in below.

 

Based on the idea of harmonic rank João M. Fernandes has created the harmonic paper count rankings of software engineering (SE) scholars using data from DBLP (date of data 3/2014). Using the harmonic paper count ranking list of Fernandes (here), I browsed through top 2000 authors to form a ranking list of "Finnish" SE scholars. To be included one has to either have a name of Finnish decent or one has to be affiliated with a Finnish organization currently or in the past.

Caveat: The list is based on visual scanning by a human (yes, me). There are likely to be omissions. Also the list is limited to forums Fernandes has considered to be part of SE.
